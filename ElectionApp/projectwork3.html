Candidates' Manifestos

These are the candidates that are currently contesting for the positions within the Student general election. Here, students can scrutinize and understand the ideas and thoughts, the candidates have, for a better administrative year once elected into power. Voters must be able to find candidates with whom they share common interests and choose wisely candidates come the Election day.

President Vice President Secretary Treasurer

Betty Ainomugisha

"If we did the things we are capable of, we would astound ourselves." ~Thomas Edison

I believe once voted into power, I will drive the student body to the greatness we were intended to achieve. I believe in a strong student body. I believe in a strong Makerere

Kenneth Ntende

"All our dreams can come true - if we have the courage to pursue them." ~Walt Disney

Democratize the Student Representative System: Re-organize, Reform and Structure!
    Re-evaluate the role of the student rep to make it more dependable and responsible. Enhance transparency and communication in the entire student rep initiative. Create a framework for reps to gather and deliver information from and to students. Improve representation for postgraduate on academic matters.

John

"Success is never final. Failure is never fatal. It is courage that counts." ~Winston Churchill

Academic Feedback & Mitigating circumstances:
    Standardise feedback for all graded assignments. Anonymous marking for essays. Campaign to stress the absolute importance for exam feedback across the years. Obtain and publicize the rules and regulations for mitigating circumstances

Pedro

"The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams." ~Eleanor Roosevelt

Course awareness & Personal advising:
    End of year presentations by professors for all upcoming courses.Re-visit and better implement the Personal Advising System.
Facilities:
    Make subject librarians accessible and known to all students. Campaign for the recording of lectures and make them accessible via moodle. Unbounded study packs for disabled students

Juan

"Whatever you can do, or dream you can, begin it. Boldness has genius, power, and magic in it." ~Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

To deliver a strong engagement with the struggle for women’s empowerment within the school, to ensure serious measures are taken against any form of gender disrespect or discrimination.To tackle of inequality beyond the campus such as challenging violence and sexism against women in society and strengthening in the fight against austerity and government cuts, as they are disproportionately affecting women.

Okotel Joshua

"What lies behind us and what lies before us are tiny matters compared to what lies within us." ~Oliver Wendell Holmes

Enable disabled students to fully participate in student politics It can be hard for disabled students to stand on a picket line or take part in a march, so we would encourage alternative platforms for activism.

Scott

Courage is doing what you're afraid to do. There can be no courage unless you're scared." ~Eddie Rickenbacker

Encourage more support groups Support groups are great for students to empathise with others and share experiences. We would love to aid people in forming new support groups e.g. for anxiety, depression and fatigue. We look forward to seeing what Chronic Support Group, the new Cancer Support Group and Student Minds have to offer

Roy

"Quit now, you'll never make it. If you disregard this advice, you'll be halfway there." ~David Zucker

To increase the awareness of our Students’ Union and what we do.To develop the community spirit within our university and increase our reputation throughout East Africa.To increase the communication between the Students’ Union and the student body
